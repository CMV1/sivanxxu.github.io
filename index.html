Who We Are

We are team 19498 — Devolotics! We started out as a team of scattered recruits from across the school, and are now a team of close friends. Along our journey, we discovered the importance of knowing our team members well, splitting our workload to tailor each member's strengths. We've also learnt that humour is our fuel, and we can't work without laughter! We've made comedic skits on YouTube and created what seems like thousands of inside jokes.

Competitions

World Championships

We are the first robotics team in our school board to participate in the World Championships, and this was truly a once in a lifetime experience. Meeting 196 teams from all across the world gave us everlasting memories and for future alumni of this team, we hope we can continue to bring this team to Worlds.

Ontario Provincial Championships

The Ontario Provincial Championships were the highest level of competition of the season yet, deciding which 2 teams of the 80 in Ontario get to go to the World Championships in Houston, Texas. This year, the Provincial Championships (or "provs", as we call it), was graciously hosted by Centennial College, where Devolotics won the coveted Inspire Award, qualifying us for the World Championships in April.

Kingston Qualifier

North Scarborough Qualifier

The Kingston Qualifier on Jan. 14th was our second qualifying event of the year. Since we already qualified, our focus was to have fun and meet new people; and we did. Here, we scored the former highest non-penalty score in Canada of 224 and won the Motivate Award.

The North Scarborough Qualifier on Dec. 10th, held at our school, was our first official qualifying event of the year. Our focus was to advance to the Provincial Championships, and we did so by winning the 2nd Place Inspire Award and the Connect Award.

Fall Fiesta

Freight Frenzy

The Fall Fiesta on November 12th was a practise tournament held at our school where we could get used to a competition environment and meet new people.

Starting strong in our first ever FTC season last year, we ranked first and second in our two qualifying matches and eighth in the provincial championships. We also won the Think and Design awards.

Our Robot

After learning the basics of robotics our rookie season (Freight Frenzy) last year, we were all excited to get our hands dirty and to go all-in for PowerPlay. We implemented many newly-learned techniques and technologies from 3D-printing to motion profiling, went through a thorough design process, and finally produced a working robot — the Hazard. We're incredibly proud that the result of our hard work will represent Canada in Houston.

Outreach

Robotics isn't all about the robots. This year we've dedicated a great deal of time and effort into educating our community about STEM. We created the Scarborough Stem Alliance (SSA), a non-profit organization aimed at inspiring youth with STEM, and through this organization we've hosted numerous robotics and programming workshops at different local schools and our local public library. We believe that the next generation relies on STEM, so we hope to empower today's students to learn all about it.
